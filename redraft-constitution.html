Redraft League Constitution

Official rules and operating procedures for VBP Fantasy Network redraft leagues.

Quick League Summary

The VBP Fantasy Network Redraft League is a 12-team seasonal fantasy football league hosted on Sleeper. It uses a Progressive PPR scoring model, runs a Weeks 1–14 regular season, and sends six teams to the playoffs in Weeks 15–17.

Managers are expected to maintain competitive lineups, follow FAAB waiver rules, and play within a structure designed for consistency, fairness, and transparency across all redraft leagues in the network.

Constitution

Use the sections below to review league rules before joining.

League Format

This document establishes the standard rules and operating procedures for all Redraft leagues within the VBP Fantasy Network.

Individual leagues may vary in buy-in amount, but all gameplay settings and governance rules follow this constitution unless explicitly stated otherwise.

  • League Size: 12 teams
  • Platform: Sleeper
  • Divisions: None
  • Matchups: One matchup per week
Season Structure
  • Regular Season: Weeks 1–14
  • Playoff Teams: 6
  • First-Round Byes: Top 2 seeds
  • Week 15: Quarterfinals
  • Week 16: Semifinals
  • Week 17: Championship
  • Trade Deadline: End of Week 11
Rosters

Starting Lineup

  • 1 QB
  • 2 RB
  • 2 WR
  • 1 TE
  • 2 FLEX (RB / WR / TE)

Bench and IR

  • Bench: 6 spots
  • IR: 1 slot
  • Players may only be placed in IR when they have an eligible injury designation on Sleeper.

Positions Not Used

  • No kickers
  • No team defense / special teams
  • No superflex
Scoring

Redraft leagues use a Progressive PPR scoring model.

  • RB: 0.5 points per reception
  • WR: 0.25 points per reception
  • TE: 0.75 points per reception

All other scoring categories follow standard Sleeper scoring settings. There are no yardage bonuses or big play bonuses. Tight ends receive no premium beyond the Progressive PPR values listed above.

Waivers
  • System: FAAB
  • Budget: $200 per team
  • Minimum Bid: None
  • Managers may submit $0 bids.
  • Waivers follow Sleeper’s default weekly waiver processing schedule.
  • After waivers process, unclaimed players become free agents until the next waiver period.
Trades

Trades are permitted until the trade deadline.

  • Trades may only be vetoed by the commissioner.
  • A veto will only occur if there is clear evidence of collusion or abuse of the league system.
  • Trades will not be vetoed due to perceived imbalance or manager strategy.
Draft Rules
  • Platform: Sleeper
  • Draft Order: Generated using 100yardrush.com
  • Format: Snake draft
  • Timing: Typically after the conclusion of the NFL preseason unless unanimously agreed otherwise
  • Speed: Sleeper fast draft setting unless changed before the draft
Competitive Integrity

Managers are expected to set a complete and competitive lineup each week before games begin.

  • Intentionally leaving starting positions empty or starting known inactive players without reasonable cause is prohibited.
  • First lineup offense: Warning from the commissioner
  • Second lineup offense: Removal from the league
  • Intentional losing is not permitted.
  • Deliberately setting a lineup designed to lose may result in intervention or removal.

Vacant Teams

  • A replacement manager may assume control and is not required to pay the entry fee.
  • If no replacement is available, the commissioner will set weekly lineups using the highest projected point total from the existing roster.
  • No trades, waiver claims, or roster moves will be made for vacant teams.
Buy-In and Prize Pool

Each league sets a buy-in amount prior to the season. Total prize pool equals Buy-In × 12 Teams.

  • League dues are collected and distributed through LeagueSafe.
  • The LeagueSafe payment link will be pinned in each league chat.
  • Managers who fail to pay by the designated deadline will be replaced prior to the season.
Prize Distribution
  • Champion: Remaining prize pool
  • 2nd Place: 2 × Buy-In
  • 3rd Place: Buy-In + $5
  • Regular Season Winner: Buy-In + $5

The additional $5 above the buy-in offsets LeagueSafe withdrawal fees. The Champion payout is reduced accordingly to maintain the total prize pool balance.

Regular Season Winner

The Regular Season Winner is the team finishing first in the standings at the conclusion of Week 14, before the playoffs begin. Standings are determined by Sleeper.

Rule Changes

Major rule changes may only occur during the offseason.

  • Any change affecting league structure, scoring, roster construction, playoff format, or prize distribution requires majority league approval.
  • The league year officially begins once draft order has been generated using 100yardrush.com.
  • After that point, rules remain locked until the following offseason.
Commissioner Authority

The commissioner may make minor administrative adjustments when necessary to ensure the league operates smoothly.

  • Clarifying written rules
  • Correcting errors or omissions in the constitution
  • Adjusting deadlines or communication procedures
  • Implementing small operational improvements

Administrative adjustments will be communicated before implementation and may not alter core league structure.
