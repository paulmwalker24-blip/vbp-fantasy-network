Best Ball Union Constitution

Official rules for the Best Ball Union format across the VBP Fantasy Network.

Quick League Summary

The Best Ball Union is a multi-league 10-team format played on Sleeper. Managers draft once, do not set weekly lineups, and compete both for their individual league title and for an overall high-score competition across the full BBU network.

The format is built for simple weekly management, cross-league competition, and scalable prize structures that can also support weekly prizes once enough leagues are active.

Constitution

Use the sections below to review league rules before joining.

League Format
  • League Type: Best Ball
  • League Size: 10 teams per league
  • Platform: Sleeper
  • Managers draft once and do not set weekly starting lineups.
  • Best Ball scoring automatically counts the highest-scoring valid lineup each week.
Season Structure
  • The regular fantasy season determines each league’s champion.
  • There are no weekly lineup submissions.
  • Teams also compete for an overall high-score pool across all Best Ball Union leagues.
Roster and Scoring

Each league uses a standard best ball build where optimal points are automatically counted each week.

  • No sit/start decisions are required.
  • Scoring follows the established league settings in Sleeper.
  • Weekly and season-long scoring still matter because of the overall competition across leagues.
Cross-League Competition

Each team competes in two ways:

  • Against the other nine teams in its own league
  • Against all teams in the Best Ball Union for overall high score

This structure lets managers win their own league while also chasing the broader BBU prize pool.

Weekly Prize Structure

Weekly prizes activate only when enough leagues are live to fund the full 17-week season.

  • If active, the highest-scoring team across all leagues for that week wins the prize.
  • If multiple teams tie, the weekly prize is split evenly.
Prize Distribution

Each Best Ball Union league contains 10 teams paying $10 each, creating a $100 league prize pool.

Standard Model

  • 75% to league winners
  • 25% to overall high score pool

Weekly Prize Model

  • 70% to league winners
  • 20% to overall high score pool
  • 10% to weekly prize pool
Weekly Prize Thresholds
  • 9 leagues: $5 per week
  • 17 leagues: $10 per week
  • 26 leagues: $15 per week
  • 34 leagues: $20 per week

If the pool exceeds a threshold but not the next level, extra funds roll into the overall high score pool.
