# Facebook Post Ideas

## Positioning

- Treat `VBP Fantasy Network` as an active brand, not a project that is still being built.
- For Facebook recruiting posts, lead with the exact openings that are live or about to open.
- Name the openings specifically before switching into broader network language.
- After the specific opening callout, widen the post into the larger multi-format network pitch.
- Keep the post short enough to scan easily in-feed.
- End with a simple CTA like `Comment or DM me if interested.`

## Preferred Logic

Use this structure for short Facebook recruiting posts:

1. Lead with the most urgent or limited openings first.
2. Mention any near-term opening that is expected soon if it is real and likely.
3. Mention newly launched leagues if they help widen the offer.
4. Shift into the broader `VBP Fantasy Network` pitch.
5. End with the site link and a direct CTA.

## Working Pattern

### Specific Openings First

`VBP Fantasy Network currently has two redraft leagues with just 1 spot left each. I should also have 1 opening coming soon in a year 2 dynasty league, and I just opened two new 12-team keeper leagues at $25 and $50.`

`If you're looking for organized fantasy football leagues with clear rules and multiple formats, check it out here: https://vbp-fantasy-network.vercel.app/`

`Comment or DM me if interested.`

## Reusable Template

`VBP Fantasy Network currently has [specific opening 1], [specific opening 2], and [new or upcoming opening].`

`If you're looking for organized fantasy football leagues with clear rules and multiple formats, check it out here: https://vbp-fantasy-network.vercel.app/`

`Comment or DM me if interested.`

## Notes For Future Posts

- The first sentence should usually be the strongest sentence in the post.
- If two limited-spot leagues are featured on the homepage, mention them explicitly.
- If a league is not truly open yet, phrase it as `should have 1 opening coming soon` instead of claiming it is open.
- If new formats launch, mention them after the urgent openings so the post feels current.
- Keep the broader network description secondary, not first.
